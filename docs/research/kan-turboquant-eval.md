# KAN / TurboQuant evaluation — honest three-bucket verdict

> Status: research verdict, 2026-06-10. Inputs: the repo as of the M5–M19 + L2.0/L2.1 CI-green state; the KAN-FPGA post (aarushgupta.io, 2026-06-07) and its two papers; the TurboQuant paper and its critique; the kernel-ML prior-art canon; The Machine retrospective. Judged against the three gates: (1) serves the documented thesis vs decorates it; (2) provable under the existing gates (framekernel, tb-encode+Kani, TCG CI); (3) right-sized vs the L2/M20 queue.

## 0. The reality being judged against

What the relevant subsystems actually compute today (no flattery):

- **M16 "inference"** is one XOR — `token = prompt ^ 0xA110_C0DE` over a u64 scalar against a static mock route table (`crates/tb-hal/src/infer.rs:188-206`). Variable-length payloads and real adapters are explicitly deferred to userspace/driver-VM providers (`infer.rs:18-26`).
- **M17 "consolidation"** is integer dedup/digest/decay bookkeeping over u64 scalar records: token-cluster distill, a rotate-xor reflect digest, and a four-threshold AND demote sweep (BLA < −1000 ∧ importance < 8 ∧ utility < 600 ∧ age ≥ 16), all i64 fixed-point SCALE=1000 (`tb-hal/src/mem.rs:693-861`).
- **M18 "evolve"** scores a one-u64 skill body by integer-hash equality against a kernel-private held-out set (`tb-encode/src/memscore.rs:88-94`, `mem.rs:1056-1072`).
- **There is no tensor, no matrix, no vector, no quantized weight anywhere in the tree.** The only field named `embedding` is a u64 scalar (`mem.rs:360, 917`); MEMORY-SPEC.md:49/:63 designs dense retrieval as a pluggable **userspace** seam.
- **Numerics are compiler-enforced integer:** both custom targets disable FP/SIMD (`-neon,-fp-armv8` / `+soft-float`), no FP state is context-switched, and the established precedent for all in-kernel value math is deterministic fixed-point with documented reachable-input envelopes and Kani panic/overflow/bounds proofs (`tb-encode/src/memscore.rs:28-39`, `proofs.rs`, `scripts/verify-encode.sh` fail-closed at `EXPECTED_HARNESSES=24`).
- **The queue** (docs/BACKLOG.md): P0s are the nested-VMX validation substrate and the L2 sovereignty chain; the thesis-critical open gap is durability (#22 VirtioBlkStore — "persistent" is currently RAM-only); real inference adapters (#24) and benchmarks/docs follow. M19 is the last cumulative marker; M20 is free.

The architectural line every proposal must respect: *"the kernel guarantees the store, the index, the quotas, the consistency and the provenance; the intelligence that decides what matters lives in pluggable userspace services"* (MEMORY-SPEC.md:10; the LLM-agnosticity theorem, ARCHITECTURE.md:122). Model computation lives on the far side of that line.

## 1. KAN — split verdict

"KAN" is three proposals wearing one name. They get three different verdicts.

### 1a. KAN as a network architecture in the kernel — **NOT NOW**

The corrected literature is unambiguous: under controlled parameter/FLOP budgets, MLPs beat KANs everywhere except symbolic-formula regression, and the ablation attributes even that win to the B-spline activation rather than the architecture ([Yu, Yu, Wang, arxiv 2407.16674](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.16674)); KAN hardware implementations cost substantially more resources for no classification gain ([Tran et al., arxiv 2407.17790](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.17790)); the original paper concedes ~10× slower training ([Liu et al., arxiv 2404.19756](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.19756)). On the TABOS side there is no input a network could consume (u64 scalars), no decision that needs one, and the in-kernel ML record is thin and specific (see §3): every success is a tiny integer model or a kernel-side formula tuned from outside. A KAN network in the kernel would be decoration, and it would have to argue — against ARCHITECTURE.md:122 — why it is "protection" rather than "intelligence." It cannot.

### 1b. A fixed-point KAN cell as a Kani-proven tb-encode encoder — **NEAR-TERM RUNG** (conditional)

This is the one configuration where the literature and the TABOS discipline genuinely click, and it deserves the serious treatment:

- **The math fits the proof system unusually well.** A KAN edge's defining property is partition-of-unity boundedness: the output is a convex combination of stored coefficients, so its range is bounded by the coefficient range *independent of input magnitude* (Liu et al.). That is precisely the input-independent envelope style tb-encode's harnesses already prove (`kani_bla_raw_panic_free_bounded` asserts |r| ≤ 34,000; `kani_minmax_in_scale_range` asserts [0,SCALE]). For a degenerate order-1 cell (linear interpolation between i16 knots), the bound is *unconditional* — clamping makes even the input assume unnecessary, which is **stronger** than the existing envelope harnesses.
- **The deployable artifact is already integer.** KANELÉ ([Hoang*, Gupta*, Harris, FPGA 2026 Best Paper, arxiv 2512.12850](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.12850)) shows the trick: quantize the *complete* learned univariate activation into one fixed-point table; inference is index + lookup + adds — no basis evaluation, no multiplies. In software under the tb-encode rules that is an `[i16; G+1]` knot table, constant-divisor indexing, i64 accumulation: `#![no_std]`, zero-dep, panic-free by construction, unwind-bounded (K ≤ 4 inputs).
- **Novelty — verified and narrowed.** Formal verification of KAN *properties* exists: [arxiv 2602.06737](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06737) (Feb 2026) verifies trained KANs by piecewise-affine abstraction + MILP, and [MonoKAN](https://www.aimodels.fyi/papers/arxiv/monokan-certified-monotonic-kolmogorov-arnold-network) certifies monotonicity. Quantized-KAN inference exists empirically ([KANtize, arxiv 2603.17230](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.17230v1); [LUT-KAN, arxiv 2601.03332](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.03332)). What does **not** appear to exist anywhere: machine-checked verification of an *executable integer KAN inference implementation* — panic/overflow-freedom plus unconditional output bounds proven over the exact code a kernel runs. A Kani-proven `kancell` module would be a genuine first **at the implementation level**, and must be claimed exactly that narrowly: one cell, not a network; safety/bounds, not accuracy.
- **It converts a documented claim into a mechanism.** Today "policy lives in userspace" is rhetoric backed by an absence (no policy hooks). A kernel-side bounds-proven evaluator whose coefficient tables are installed from userspace through a rights-gated, fail-closed method is the exokernel separation made executable: the kernel still understands nothing about *what matters* — it enforces only the envelope. That passes gate (1) honestly.

**The rung** (one milestone, marker `M20: kan-policy OK`): see the proposal section — `crates/tb-encode/src/kancell.rs` (G=8 segments over [0,1000], K=4, ~3 new harnesses, `EXPECTED_HARNESSES` 24→27), M17 op-selector widening (op=9 INSTALL_POLICY_TABLE under `Rights::CONSOLIDATE`, op=10 digest witness), per-component transform-then-threshold in `forget_sweep` with identity defaults bit-identical to current behavior, zero new unsafe, zero new ABI method numbers. Design honesty note: an additive sum-then-threshold GAM cannot reproduce the existing 4-way AND — so each component keeps its threshold and gains a learned transform in front of it; do not pretend otherwise.

**What it does not prove, stated up front:** that learned consolidation beats the fixed thresholds. There is no workload to train against yet; default tables are identity, and the selftest's non-identity table is a deterministic witness of the *seam*, exactly as M16's mock witnessed the provider contract. Kani proves safety and bounds, never numerical accuracy — accuracy of any future trained table lives in the concrete-vector Miri lane (the #49 over-quantification trap applies to monotonicity too, `proofs.rs:300-304`).

**Sequencing:** behind the standing P0s (nested-VMX substrate) and not displacing durability (#22) — the persistence pillar being RAM-only is a bigger honesty gap than any missing evaluator. The rung is host-heavy and small, so it can ride alongside; it must not jump the queue.

### 1c. KAN on FPGA / sub-microsecond online learning — **VISION MATERIAL**

The 2700× and sub-microsecond numbers ([arxiv 2602.02056](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02056)) are spatial-parallelism results; on a CPU a KAN edge costs ~(S+1)× an MLP weight's work, and the post's own conclusion concedes the properties are hard to exploit off-FPGA. TABOS's entire device-I/O capability is a polled virtio-rng (M19). The correct disposition is one vision-doc paragraph: TABOS's proven-bounded fixed-point policy evaluators are exactly the artifact class that compiles to KANELÉ-style LUTs if programmable-logic/near-memory compute ever arrives — recorded as upside, never premise. Spline locality (O(S+1) coefficient updates per observation) is noted as the cheapest known gradient-shaped update *if* a concrete non-stationary kernel workload ever demands in-kernel adaptation; none does today.

## 2. TurboQuant — **NOT NOW**

[TurboQuant (arxiv 2504.19874)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.19874) is data-oblivious online vector quantization (random rotation → per-coordinate Lloyd-Max-against-Beta scalar quantization → optional 1-bit QJL residual for unbiased inner products), near-optimal at all bit-widths, strongest on KV-cache compression and ANN recall. Four reasons it fails the gates today:

1. **No use case in the tree.** There are no embedding vectors anywhere; the dense T3 channel is designed as a userspace/provider seam (MEMORY-SPEC.md:49, :63). Embedding quantization is "intelligence/policy," and the documents place it outside the kernel.
2. **Wrong scale.** A 12-agent, single-node, RAM-only store under a 2^20 token quota has zero compression pressure. When embeddings first arrive, 8-bit scalar quantization is adequate and simpler — adopt low-bit only when measured pressure exists.
3. **Implementability is conditional.** The paper-exact dense float rotation is unrepresentable under the soft-float targets. The family's standard substitution — randomized Hadamard transform (adds/subs + sign flips, log2(d) bit growth, i16→i32) plus fixed-point threshold tables — *would* fit the tb-encode discipline, but that is an argument about a future, not a need.
4. **Credibility risk.** The RaBitQ first author's public critique ([Gao, dev.to, Mar 2026](https://dev.to/gaoj0017/turboquant-and-rabitq-what-the-public-story-gets-wrong-1i00)) — structural overlap obscured, prior optimal bounds mischaracterized, an A100-vs-single-threaded-Python comparison — is unresolved; the brand is also surrounded by SEO/AI-content amplification claiming numbers absent from the paper. Specs should name the **rotation+scalar-quantization family** (with RaBitQ as the better-documented reference), never the brand.

One paragraph of the pattern earns vision-doc status for the future dense tier (see vision material); everything else waits for the channel to exist.

## 3. The prior-art bar (why the verdicts land where they do)

The in-kernel ML record, honestly read: **LinnOS** (OSDI 2020) — 3-layer *integer-quantized* NN, binary decision, 4–6 µs against ~80 µs SSDs, authors flag it breaks as devices get faster. **KML** (HotStorage 2021/TOS 2023) — tiny in-kernel models for two knobs, <4KB, real gains, and a demonstration of how much engineering two knobs cost. **Google far memory** (ASPLOS 2019, in production) — the strongest pattern: the kernel hot path executes a dirt-cheap deterministic formula; the learning (GP bandit) lives offline and re-tunes coefficients. **Learned indexes** — the sober endpoint is PGM (PVLDB 2020): provable error bounds with piecewise-*linear* fits, no NN. **ghOSt** (SOSP 2021) — delegate policy to userspace entirely. **LAKE** (ASPLOS 2023) — why heavyweight in-kernel ML doesn't pay. The converged rules: tiny + integer-only; cheap-to-be-wrong decisions; gated operation 10–100× slower than inference; learning off the hot path. (Note: the "Kraken" sometimes cited in this space is a mis-citation — the real Krakens are a userspace recommender-training system, SC 2020, and Meta's load-testing system, OSDI 2020; neither is kernel ML.)

TABOS's existing design — deterministic, Kani-proved fixed-point formulas over bounded inputs — already *is* the converged pattern, and is ahead of all of the above on verification (none proves overflow-freedom). The prior-art-sanctioned next steps are exactly two: control-plane tuning of formula coefficients (the far-memory pattern), and quantized learned 1D transform tables (LinnOS-style integer inference with KAN-style boundedness for the proofs). The M20 rung is the second, shaped to also serve the first. A general in-kernel network, in-kernel training, or a learned hot-path scorer replacement would all be regressions against this record.

## 4. The Machine alignment (honest trajectory statement)

The Machine's failure mode: a whole-stack rewrite contingent on unshipped memory hardware (memristors pulled 2015; the 2017 160TB prototype ran battery-backed DRAM; the program faded after 2018; the fabric DNA survived only as Gen-Z→CXL, Feb 2022). Its durable residue was the *software* abstraction: one persistent addressable substrate, computation brought to the data. TABOS claims exactly that residue, in software, on commodity hardware: agent memory as the primary kernel-guaranteed substrate with recall/consolidation/forgetting as first-class deterministic kernel operations — true and CI-green today, with two asterisks that must stay visible: persistence is RAM-only pending #22, and payloads are u64 scalars. The KAN/quant additions change this trajectory only at the margin (a proven-bounded policy-evaluator substrate strengthens the measurable part of the claim); the FPGA/fabric/CXL material is upside prose, never premise. The discipline The Machine enforces: benchmarks against the boring baseline (conventional kernel + userspace vector store), and no pillar contingent on hardware that hasn't shipped.
