## 1. Why this milestone, and why these choices

M31 put real model traffic on the channel; M32 (proposed) gives the machine its own local mind; M13/M20 gives it verified lexical memory; M18 gave it a capability-gated approval seam; M22 gives it a tamper-evident decision ledger. Each is an **organ in isolation**. What Yuva has never done is **orchestrate** them — select an organ, assign it a role, run a Verifier-gated loop, and produce a measured, verifiable result. That is the binding lesson of the TRINITY analysis (§5): the verdict is not "you were right about role-delegation"; it is "wire the verified organ into a real loop and MEASURE something." M38 is that wiring.

Three places a naive "build an agent loop" milestone would rot, each fixed by construction:

1. **The orchestration policy must be on the verified, no-float side of the kernel boundary — or it is not Yuva's.** TRINITY's actual mechanism is a CMA-ES-trained float policy. CMA-ES is intrinsically Gaussian/covariance/float (§9) — it cannot live in a Kani-provable `no_std`/`forbid(unsafe_code)`/no-float leaf. The resolution is the cogi-doc ADOPT-2 separation applied pre-emptively: **the conductor is a HAND-WRITTEN discrete policy** (TRINITY itself rates ADOPT-1 "High feasibility … can be a hand-written verified policy first, no ES needed"); a learned policy, if ever adopted, is trained off-substrate and distilled into a Kani-verifiable fixed-point artifact (§9, named-deferred). The decision ALGEBRA — role totality, bounded turns, Verifier-gates-termination, every-transition-attested — is Kani-proven exactly as `tpsched.rs` proves the two-VMID partition algebra (`token=policy=DISCRETE-HAND-WRITTEN-NOT-LEARNED`).

2. **The Verifier must be a real fail-closed GATE, not a no-op — or the loop is hollow — AND the proposal must name the gate that ACTUALLY bites in the required chain.** A stub conductor that always picks one organ and always accepts is the M22-demotion class of forgery. The honest decomposition (re-grounded against the code — see §3 and the §Adversarial-review must-fix):
   - In the **required all-mock CI chain**, NO organ is high-impact (`RetrievalOverMemory`, `LocalM32`-mock, `ExternalMock` are all non-high-impact), so the M18.1 `harness_merge` gate's fail-closed `Denied` branch (caps.rs:1003-1008, taken ONLY when `high == true`) is **NEVER reached** — it reduces to the `high==false → skip` no-op every boot. The Verifier that **actually bites in CI** is therefore the discrete `bakeoff::gate_clears`-shaped verdict (`bakeoff.rs:494`, `ACTIVATION_MARGIN=250`) over the Worker organ's output digest — hand-written, Kani-verified, conjunctive ACCEPT/REVISE, **never a learned classifier**.
   - The **runtime organ-invocation** itself is gated by the landed `INVOKE_MODEL` **possession-of-right** check (`required_right(M_MODEL_INVOKE_BYTES) → Rights::INVOKE_MODEL`, caps.rs:294, enforced at caps.rs:649) — a possession gate, NOT a human-approval gate.
   - The M18.1 `harness_merge` human-approval gate composes in ONLY for the distinct event of **ADMITTING** a high-impact organ/skill (its actual landed role: a one-time PROPOSED→ADMITTED merge requiring an `APPROVE_HIGH_IMPACT` capability, used at the skill-merge site) — i.e., at the **operator-gated stage** when a high-impact (live/external) organ is admitted, NOT on every loop step in the mock chain.
   - Any per-STEP human-approval interposition on organ INVOCATION (as opposed to admission) would be an **HONESTLY-NAMED NEW kernel seam** over the `INVOKE_MODEL`/`EMIT_EXTERNAL` `required_right` checks — it is NOT "verbatim reuse of `harness_merge`." The required chain does not need it; it is named-deferred to the operator-gated stage (§7, §9).

   `token=verifier=GATE-CI-BITES-DISCRETE-VERDICT/M18-ADMISSION-GATE-ONLY-AT-OPERATOR-STAGE`, `token=invoke=INVOKE_MODEL-POSSESSION-GATE`, `token=per-step-approval=NEW-SEAM-NAMED-DEFERRED`.

3. **The result must be MEASURED, not architected — and the measurement must be stub-resistant by INDEPENDENT HOST RECOMPUTE, not by printed numbers.** The DoD is not "a conductor exists"; it is a deterministic boot self-test that RUNS the loop on a fixed transcript and prints a measured trace, where the **host independently recomputes the M22 fold from a SEPARATE capture of the organ-call trace** (the load-bearing stub-killer; printed `organs`/`revise-cycles` numbers are NOT trusted, since a stub can print them — §8). Anti-hollow is load-bearing (§8): the fixed task REQUIRES a measured ≥2-organ sequence with ≥1 Verifier `REVISE`-then-`ACCEPT` cycle, where `organs` and `revise-cycles` are each tied to **DISTINCT folded `ConductDecision` records the host re-derives** (so a stub printing `organs=0x2 revise-cycles=0x1 head=<const>` over FABRICATED decisions fails the independent-recompute leg), and the ADOPT-4 cost record (organ-calls/turns/logical-ticks) is folded tamper-evidently — TRINITY's biggest hidden weakness turned into a Yuva invariant (`token=cost=HONESTLY-ACCOUNTED-TOKENED`, `token=stub-resistance=HOST-RECOMPUTE-FROM-INDEPENDENT-TRACE`).

**And one thing M38 deliberately is:** *the small-orchestrator vision, made buildable* — a tiny verified scheduler that composes large organs, exactly the operator's "<20K-param coordinator beats the monolith" thesis, but with the four things TRINITY threw away bolted on: the organs retrieve over **verified tamper-evident memory** (M13/M20), the decisions fold into a **tamper-evident lineage** (M22), the policy is **verified** (Kani), and the organs are **sovereign-or-gated** (M32-local / M18-admission-gated). The novelty is the wrapper, never a learning advance.

The silicon (timer PPI, `VTTBR_EL2`) stays in `tb-hal`. **This IS the policy/execution split for the VM scheduler — the Conductor is its organ-selection twin.**
- **M16 `route.rs`** is a LANDED verified discrete "which-backend-next" decision: `parse_scheme` (`route.rs:35`) + `longest_prefix_index` (`route.rs:58`), total/panic-free over UNTRUSTED input, no-float, Miri-lane-verified. **This is literally a deterministic organ-router precedent** — the conductor's organ pick reuses its shape.
- **M31/M32 execution ALREADY lives host-side:** `tools/xport-harness/src/{main,live}.rs` + `tb-vmm/src/infer_host.rs` do the network/model calls; CI runs only `backend=MOCK-DETERMINISTIC`; live Anthropic is operator-gated via `real-infer.yml` (`workflow_dispatch` ONLY). ### 2.1 The verified policy leaf — `crates/tb-encode/src/conductor.rs`

A new `tb-encode::conductor` leaf (`no_std`, `forbid(unsafe_code)`, no-float, zero-alloc, zero-dep, Kani-proven), lifted exactly as `tpsched.rs` was lifted from `exittel.rs`. Contents:

- **The role vocabulary** — `enum Role { Thinker, Worker, Verifier }` as a closed `u8` enum (mirroring `exp.rs::OutcomeLabel`). The three-role taxonomy is TRINITY's verbatim.
- **The organ vocabulary** — `enum Organ { RetrievalOverMemory = 0x00, LocalM32 = 0x01, ExternalMock = 0x02 }` as a closed `u8` enum mirroring the `inferwire::peer` byte discipline. In CI `ExternalMock` is the M31 **MOCK-DETERMINISTIC** backend standing in for the external organ — never a live byte. (`peer::INFER_DAEMON_PURE`-style reserved ids leave room for the live external organ at the operator-gated stage, §7.)
- **The verdict** — `enum Verdict { Accept, Revise, HaltBudget }`.
- **`select_organ(state) -> Organ`** — a total, panic-free, deterministic pick over a fixed registered-organ slice, in the shape of `route::longest_prefix_index` (deterministic lowest-index tie-break, fail-closed out-of-range).
- **`assign_role(state) -> Role`** — total/deterministic role assignment.
- **`next(turn: u8, role: Role, verdict: Verdict) -> Action`** — the FIXED bounded transition table, total + panic-free, in the shape of `tpsched::next_slot` (round-robin successor, fail-closed). The loop step terminates at `min{ k <= MAX_TURNS : role_k = Verifier ∧ verdict_k = Accept }`, else `HaltBudget` at `MAX_TURNS = 5` — never a silent infinite loop, never a silent success.
- **The discrete Verifier verdict** — a `gate_clears`-shaped conjunctive ACCEPT/REVISE over the Worker organ's output digest (`bakeoff.rs:494` shape, `ACTIVATION_MARGIN`), hand-written and Kani-verified — this is the Verifier that bites in the required mock chain (§1.2, §3).
- **`ConductDecision { turn: u8, role: u8, organ: u8, verdict: u8, organ_calls: u16, t_logical: u64 }`** — the fixed-width injective decision record, with `canon`/`canon_len`/`decode` mirroring `tpsched::SchedDecision` (`tpsched.rs:113-160`, `SCHED_CANON_LEN`). The `organ_calls` field is the ADOPT-4 cost token, folded in-record (§5).
- **The fold REUSES the M22 prov leaf verbatim** — `conductor.rs` re-exports `crate::prov::{append, chain_mix, recompute, verify_inclusion, head_witness, PROV_HASH_LEN}` exactly as `tpsched.rs:34-39` does, under a **NEW `prov::kind::CONDUCT_DECISION` tag** beside `WRITE = 1`/`FORGET = 2`/`SKILL_ADMIT = 3` (`prov.rs:78-85`). **Zero new fold math** — exactly as M23/M25/M26/M27 reuse it.

## 3. The Thinker/Worker/Verifier loop — provable termination, the honestly-named Verifier composition

The loop is a FIXED bounded state machine, the M27 `next_slot` shape generalized from two slots to a role×organ×verdict transition. State = `(turn: u8, role: Role, organ: Organ, verdict: Verdict)`. The transition `next(...)` is total over the small closed set (the same total/panic-free/deterministic-tie-break property as `route::longest_prefix_index` and `tpsched::next_slot`).

**Termination (the PROVABLE property — Kani harness §6).** Two hard guarantees:
- **Bounded turns:** for ALL inputs the turn counter is monotone and never exceeds `MAX_TURNS = 5` (TRINITY's K). Proven loop-free or under a small `#[kani::unwind(6)]`.
- **Verifier-gates-termination:** the ONLY `Accept`-terminal transition has `role = Verifier`. A non-Verifier ACCEPT cannot terminate the loop; at `MAX_TURNS` without a Verifier-ACCEPT the verdict is `HaltBudget` (fail-closed), never silent-success and never silent-loop.

This is exactly the `tpsched` liveness discipline (`next_slot` has no fixed point → no VMID starves) lifted to the conductor: no `(role, organ)` is a fixed point that starves progress (`token=loop=BOUNDED-VERIFIER-GATED`, `termination=ACCEPT-OR-HALT-BUDGET`).

**The Verifier composition — honestly re-grounded (the §Adversarial-review central fix).** The Verifier is the conjunction of three gates, each named against the seam that ACTUALLY enforces it:

- **(A) The CI-biting discrete verdict (load-bearing in the required all-mock chain).** ACCEPT/REVISE is the discrete-verdict shape of M24 `bakeoff::gate_clears` (`bakeoff.rs:494`, `ACTIVATION_MARGIN = 250`) over the Worker organ's output digest — hand-written, Kani-verified, **never a learned classifier** (`token=learning=DORMANT (KAN_ACTIVE=false, mem/mod.rs:273)`). **This is the gate that bites every boot in the cumulative chain.**
- **(B) The `INVOKE_MODEL` possession gate (runtime organ invocation).** Each organ invocation flows through the landed `required_right(M_MODEL_INVOKE_BYTES) → Rights::INVOKE_MODEL` possession check (caps.rs:294, enforced at caps.rs:649) — fail-closed `BadCap` on an `INVOKE_MODEL`-less handle. This is the seam the agent loop's `M_MODEL_INVOKE_BYTES` step already uses (`main.rs:4226` — "the SAME INVOKE_MODEL gate"). It is a possession gate, NOT a human-approval gate.
- **(C) The M18.1 human-approval ADMISSION gate (operator-gated stage ONLY).** `CapTable::harness_merge` (`caps.rs:991`) + `carries_approval` (`caps.rs:970`) STRUCTURALLY BLOCK the **ADMISSION** of a high-impact (`EMIT_EXTERNAL`-class) organ/skill unless an `APPROVE_HIGH_IMPACT` capability is presented — but its fail-closed `Denied` branch is taken **ONLY when `high == true`** (caps.rs:999-1011). In the required all-mock chain NO organ is high-impact, so this branch is **never reached** — it is a `high==false → skip` no-op every boot. It composes in ONLY when a high-impact (live/external) organ is **admitted** at the operator-gated stage (§7). The marker `M18.1: approval-gate OK` (`kernel/src/main.rs:3123`) is the landed precedent for that admission event. **A per-STEP human-approval gate on organ INVOCATION does not exist and would be a NEW kernel seam (§1.2, §9) — it is not "verbatim reuse" of `harness_merge`, which gates admission, not invocation.**
- **(D) M22 attestation (every step).** Every transition is canon-encoded and folded into a per-session `conduct_head` via the reused prov fold; a transition that fails recompute/`verify_inclusion` is rejected.

**Exercising the inert M18 leg (the §Adversarial-review negative-test mandate).** Because gate (C) is provably inert in the required chain, M38 adds a NEGATIVE test — on a dedicated/dispatch-only lane (NOT the required cumulative chain) — that forces a **high-impact organ ADMISSION step WITHOUT approval** and asserts `harness_merge → SysStatus::Denied` (the `high==true` branch). This is a Kani-adjacent unit assertion plus a guest-serial negative-marker assertion; it ensures the M18 leg is EXERCISED at least once rather than only ASSERTED. `token=m18-leg=EXERCISED-ON-DEDICATED-LANE-NOT-REQUIRED-CHAIN`.

## 5. ADOPT-4 — honest cost-accounting as a witness invariant (the thing TRINITY omitted)

TRINITY's published numbers **omitted** the coordinator's own call/turn/latency cost — the analysis names this its biggest hidden weakness and mandates (ADOPT-4) that Yuva turn it into an invariant. M38 does so **structurally**: every `ConductDecision` carries a tokened cost field (`organ_calls`, `turn`, `t_logical` as **logical ticks — a deterministic surrogate, NOT wall-clock latency / dollars**, which a deterministic offline chain cannot and does not claim to measure; see §Adversarial-review), folded tamper-evidently into the M22 chain alongside the WRITE/SKILL_ADMIT entries (the `mem/mod.rs:1018 ledger_append` → `chain_head` field at `:913`, folded by the `chain_head()` method at `:1045`). The per-session witness emits the accumulated cost:

```
cost: organ-calls=0x<hex> turns=0x<hex> logical-ticks=0x<hex> attested=0x1
```

Because it folds through the prov leaf, the cost record is **producer-only and tamper-evident**, and — critically — it is `signal=OBSERVATIONAL-NONCAUSAL`: the conductor RECORDS its cost, it never ADAPTS its schedule from its own folded telemetry (the M24/M26 confounding firewall holds — a schedule learned from its own cost stream would re-import the confounded loop M24 refuses). The captured quantity is the **deterministic logical surrogate** (organ-calls/turns/logical-ticks), NOT the wall-clock/dollar latency TRINITY hid — Yuva captures what an offline deterministic chain CAN attest, and names the rest FRONTIER. `token=cost=HONEST-ACCOUNTED-TOKENED (ADOPT-4)`, `token=cost-metric=LOGICAL-SURROGATE-NOT-WALLCLOCK`.
